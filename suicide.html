Suicide

More than 700 000 people die due to suicide every year. For every suicide there are many more people who attempt suicide. A prior suicide attempt is an important risk factor for suicide in the general population. Suicide is the fourth leading cause of death among 15–29-year-olds. Seventy-seven per cent of global suicides occur in low- and middle-income countries. Ingestion of pesticide, hanging and firearms are among the most common methods of suicide globally.

What is the suicide?

Suicide is killing oneself, and it is a tragic reaction to stressful life situations. There is no single reason why anyone would attempt suicide. However, there are factors that increase the risk of attempting suicide. Depression is considered the main risk factor for suicide, but there are many psychological and mental disorders and risk factors. Others can contribute to suicide. Firearms are considered the most common means of suicide, in addition to other common methods such as taking an overdose of medication, suffocation, and hanging.

Suicide treatment

Treatment of suicidal thoughts and behavior depends on the patient’s situation, including his level of suicide risk and the underlying problems that may cause suicidal thoughts or behavior. The treatment methods are as follows:

Treating emergency suicide cases
  • In the event of suicide and self-destruction, you must call the emergency number, and if other people are present, you must seek help.them and contact the emergency services.
  • If there is no injury but there is a risk of exposing oneself.If there is, you should call for help and call emergency services.
Treating suicidal thoughts.

The presence of suicidal thoughts requires treatment steps that may include the following:

  • Psychotherapy for suicide that helps the patient explore the reasons why he feels suicidal and helps him learn skills to manage emotions more effectively.
  • Medications, antidepressants, antipsychotic medications and medications anti-anxiety and other medications for mental illness can help in reducing symptoms and reducing suicidal behavior.
  • Addiction treatment, which may include treatment for drug addiction or alcohol.
  • Family support and education: Family or loved ones can help for the patient to be a source of support that can help them involve them in treatment to understand what the patient is going through, and give them skills better coping, improved communication and family relationships.
