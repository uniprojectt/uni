Medicines available without a doctor's prescription

  • Examples of it
  • Its damage
  • How to prevent it
  • Alternative treatment


What is it

Non-prescription medications are medications that are dispensed without a prescription, mild pain relievers, antacids, and some ulcer and esophageal reflux medications.


Examples of it

The most important examples of non-prescribed medications: Analgesics and antipyretics Cold and cold medicines Stomach and esophageal reflux medications, sleep medications Allergy and itching medications Diarrhea medicine Constipation medicines


Its damaged

Side effects: You may have an allergic reaction or a harmful interaction with the components of the medication, or the medication may affect other organs of the body in an unexpected way. Interference with other medications you are taking and increases the risk of harmful drug interactions. It may affect the efficiency of other medications or reduce their effectiveness. Non-prescribed medications may have an unproven scientific composition or inappropriate doses, may not have the desired effect, or be ineffective in treating the disease they target. Increased risk of addiction Some non-prescription medications may have addictive properties and using them incorrectly or in isolation can increase the risk of addiction to them. The use of non-prescribed medications may interfere with your existing health conditions. There may be a negative impact on your general health condition or an increase in the symptoms you suffer from.


How to prevent it

Speed in conducting medical examination and accurate diagnostic analysis Replace antibiotics with fruits rich in vitamin C, such as oranges, tangerines, and lemons Strengthening immunity, such as eating vegetables and fruits and paying attention to a healthy, integrated diet Lots of warm drinks, such as herbs, anise, and mint Pay attention to cleanliness in food and drink to reduce the chances of contracting diseases Avoid using painkillers when feeling tired and suffice with a period of rest and sleep


Alternative treatment

1) Tell the patient about the harms of using non-prescribed medications to increase awareness of them and the need to stop using them excessively.

2) Follow the instructions for taking the medication with his eyes, and do not increase or decrease the doses without consulting the doctor first

3) Do not stop taking the medication without medical advice and do not crush or break medication pills or tablets without instructions.

4) Make sure to know the effect of the prescribed medication on daily tasks such as driving

5) Talk honestly with the doctor about your personal or family medical history

6) Do not use medications that have been prescribed for other patients without first consulting a doctor

7) Telling the patient about other solutions and alternatives to treat the health problem he faces, such as using alternative medicine, herbs, some spices, and diets.

-There are many methods that society can follow to reduce its misuse
1-Education: It is recommended to educate parents, children, and doctors who prescribe medications about the effect of prescribed medications on the brain and development.
2_Safe storage and disposal of medications. It is important to protect medications at home in a safe place, which reduces the chance of children or adolescents accessing them.
3_Monitoring medications that require a prescription: Many people are calling on doctors and pharmacists to better monitor the process of prescribing medications, which helps identify overprescription and misuse of medications.
